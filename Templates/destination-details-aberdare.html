Aberdare National Park


Africa’s Misty Highland Sanctuary

Set in Kenya’s cool central highlands along the eastern edge of the Great Rift Valley, Aberdare National Park offers a safari experience unlike any other. Smaller than many of Kenya’s savannah parks, Aberdare is defined by dramatic contrasts — from rolling moorlands and bamboo forests to deep valleys, waterfalls, and dense rainforest. At Mosmak Tours & Travel, we present Aberdare as a hidden gem for travelers seeking a quieter, scenic, and uniquely atmospheric safari.

A Landscape of Contrasts

Aberdare’s terrain ranges from high-altitude moorland plateaus to lush forested slopes. Mist often drifts across the hills, creating an almost mystical ambiance. The park’s waterfalls, including the beautiful Chania Falls, cascade dramatically into forested ravines, offering breathtaking views and memorable photography moments. The cooler climate adds to the park’s charm, making it a refreshing alternative to Kenya’s warmer lowland parks.

Exceptional Wildlife Encounters

Despite its compact size, Aberdare National Park supports an impressive variety of wildlife. Visitors may encounter Africa’s renowned Big Five — elephant, lion, leopard, buffalo, and rhino — within its forests and open areas. The park is particularly known for: One of Kenya’s largest populations of black rhinos Rare forest species such as the bongo antelope Melanistic (black) leopards Spotted hyenas and other forest-dwelling mammals Wildlife viewing here often feels intimate and exclusive, especially from elevated lodges overlooking waterholes.

A Birdwatcher’s Haven

With over 290 recorded bird species, Aberdare is a paradise for bird enthusiasts. Rare and endemic species such as the scarlet-tufted malachite sunbird and the Aberdare cisticola make every outing exciting for both seasoned birders and casual observers.

Unique Safari Experiences

Aberdare offers experiences that go beyond traditional game drives: Stay in iconic tree lodges overlooking floodlit waterholes, where wildlife visits throughout the night Enjoy guided nature walks through forest trails Try trout fishing in clear mountain streams Embark on thrilling night game drives to spot nocturnal animals Take in sweeping views of valleys and distant peaks from elevated vantage points These diverse activities make Aberdare ideal for travelers seeking variety within their safari journey.

Cultural and Conservation Significance

The Aberdare region is rich in history and cultural heritage, with nearby communities maintaining strong traditions connected to the highlands. The park also plays a vital role in protecting water catchment areas that supply much of central Kenya, highlighting its ecological importance. Through Mosmak Tours & Travel, your visit supports responsible tourism and conservation efforts that help preserve this vital ecosystem.
